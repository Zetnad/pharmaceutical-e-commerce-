MediHub HMS is operational software. It is not a substitute for professional judgment, regulatory compliance, or local institutional policies governing clinical care, billing, employment, pharmacy practice, or privacy.

Organizations should assign access based on role and scope of responsibility. Typical roles include:

  • Patients or patient-facing registration staff

Each organization is responsible for ensuring that users only perform actions allowed under local law, licensing, internal policy, and professional scope of practice.

  • Controlled medicines, high-risk procedures, and emergency actions require local safety and accountability controls.

5. AI-assisted features

MediHub HMS may offer AI-assisted outputs such as triage suggestions, note drafts, medication safety prompts, coding cues, or operational recommendations.

AI-generated outputs must be treated as support content only. They are not independent diagnoses, prescriptions, claims adjudications, staffing decisions, or final medical orders. Human review is required.

  • Users must not rely on AI outputs where professional review is required.
  • Unsafe, inaccurate, or misleading AI outputs should be reported for review and governance follow-up.
