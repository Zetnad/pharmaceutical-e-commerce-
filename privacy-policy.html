Privacy Policy

Your privacy is fundamental to our mission. Learn how we collect, use, and protect your personal and health data.

Home/Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 1, 2026 · Effective Date: February 15, 2026

MediHub is committed to protecting the privacy of all users in accordance with the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019 (DPA), the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) where applicable, and international healthcare data standards. Health data is classified as sensitive personal data and receives the highest level of protection.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Information

When you register or use MediHub, we may collect the following personal data:

  • Identity Data: Full name, date of birth, national ID or passport number, gender
  • Contact Data: Email address, phone number, physical address, delivery address
  • Account Data: Username, password (encrypted), account preferences
  • Financial Data: M-Pesa number, bank account details, payment card information (processed via PCI DSS-compliant payment processors)

1.2 Health & Pharmaceutical Data

In the course of providing pharmaceutical services, we may process:

  • Prescription Data: Prescription details, prescriber information, medication history
  • Medical History: Allergies, chronic conditions (only when voluntarily provided for drug interaction checking)
  • Pharmaceutical Purchase History: Medications purchased, dosages, frequency of orders
  • Insurance Data: Health insurance provider, policy number (where applicable)
Health-related personal data is classified as sensitive personal data under Section 2 of the Kenya DPA and Article 9 of the GDPR. We process this data only with your explicit consent and for the specific purposes outlined in this policy.

1.3 Technical Data

  • IP address, browser type and version, operating system
  • Device identifiers, screen resolution, language preferences
  • Pages visited, time spent on pages, click patterns
  • Referring website, search terms used within the Platform

2. How We Use Your Information

We process your personal data for the following purposes and legal bases:

Purpose Legal Basis (DPA 2019)
Account creation and management Performance of contract
Processing pharmaceutical orders Performance of contract
Prescription verification and dispensing Legal obligation (Pharmacy & Poisons Act)
Drug interaction and allergy checking Vital interests / Explicit consent
Payment processing Performance of contract
Delivery coordination Performance of contract
Customer support and dispute resolution Legitimate interest
Regulatory compliance and audits Legal obligation
Platform analytics and improvement Legitimate interest
Marketing communications Consent (opt-in)

3. Data Sharing & Third Parties

We share personal data only with the following categories of recipients:

  • Pharmacist Tenants: Order details and delivery information necessary to fulfill your pharmaceutical orders
  • Payment Processors: M-Pesa (Safaricom), Stripe, or other PCI DSS-compliant payment providers
  • Delivery Partners: Name, address, and phone number for order delivery
  • Regulatory Authorities: Pharmacy and Poisons Board, when required by law for controlled substance tracking
  • Law Enforcement: Only when compelled by valid court order or legal process

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties for marketing purposes. We do not share your health data with insurance companies, employers, or any unauthorized third parties.

4. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption at rest, TLS 1.3 encryption in transit
  • Access Controls: Role-based access control (RBAC), multi-factor authentication for staff
  • Data Isolation: Multi-tenant architecture with strict data isolation between pharmacy tenants
  • Audit Logging: Comprehensive audit trails for all access to sensitive health data
  • Penetration Testing: Annual third-party security audits and penetration testing
  • Incident Response: 72-hour breach notification to the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner

5. Data Retention

We retain personal data for the following periods:

  • Account data: Duration of account activity plus 2 years after account deletion
  • Transaction records: 7 years (as required by Kenya Revenue Authority)
  • Prescription records: 5 years (as required by the Pharmacy and Poisons Board)
  • Controlled substance records: 10 years (as required by the Narcotic Drugs Act)
  • Marketing consent records: Duration of consent plus 1 year

6. Your Rights

Under the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019, you have the following rights:

  • Right of Access: Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you
  • Right to Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Request limitation of how we use your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interest or for marketing
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior processing

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer at dpo@medihub.co.ke. We will respond within 30 days.

7. International Data Transfers

Your data is primarily stored on servers located in Kenya. Where data is transferred outside Kenya (e.g., for cloud hosting or analytics), we ensure adequate safeguards through Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner, adequacy decisions, and binding corporate rules.

8. Children's Privacy

MediHub is not intended for use by individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. Parents or guardians may create accounts to order medications for minor dependents under their supervision.
