Privacy Policy

How MediHub HMS handles patient, staff, billing, pharmacy, and AI-related data across hospital operations.

Home/Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 8, 2026

MediHub HMS is designed for healthcare environments and therefore handles highly sensitive data. We apply heightened safeguards to patient records, staff records, insurance details, payment information, and AI-assisted workflow data.

1. Scope of this policy

This policy explains how MediHub HMS collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects information processed through the platform. The platform may be used by hospitals, clinics, maternity centers, diagnostic sites, pharmacies, finance teams, administrators, and patients.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Patient and clinical data

  • Identity and demographic details such as name, date of birth, gender, next of kin, and patient number
  • Visit and encounter history including appointments, triage notes, diagnoses, prescriptions, allergies, and discharge summaries
  • Orders and results for laboratory, imaging, pharmacy dispensing, and procedure-related workflows
  • Insurance membership, benefit plan, guarantor, pre-authorization, claim, and remittance data

2.2 Staff and workforce data

  • Employee identity records, job titles, departments, and assigned roles
  • Licenses, certifications, roster coverage, attendance, overtime, and payroll inputs
  • Audit trail events for system access, approvals, and operational decisions

2.3 Payment and operational data

  • Invoices, cashier transactions, mobile money references, card payments, and reconciliation records
  • Medication inventory, batch numbers, expiry dates, stock movements, and controlled-register events
  • Quality, safety, and compliance logs needed for healthcare oversight

2.4 AI workflow data

  • User-entered symptom summaries, care prompts, and operational prompts
  • AI-generated draft outputs such as triage suggestions, note drafts, or medication safety prompts
  • Feedback and override events used to monitor quality and safety
AI outputs in MediHub HMS are intended to support human decision-making. They should not be treated as fully autonomous diagnoses, treatment plans, payroll decisions, or insurance adjudications.

3. How we use information

Purpose Examples
Care delivery and continuity Registration, triage, consultation, pharmacy dispensing, admission, discharge, and follow-up
Hospital operations Queue management, staffing, ward management, supply tracking, and executive reporting
Billing and payments Cashiering, patient balances, insurer claims, remittances, and reconciliation
Compliance and safety Access logging, medication accountability, quality review, and regulatory reporting
AI-assisted workflows Drafting notes, prompting triage review, highlighting documentation gaps, and surfacing medication risks

4. Data sharing

We share data only where needed for legitimate healthcare, payment, compliance, or service delivery purposes.

  • Within the care team: Access is limited by role, location, and job responsibility.
  • With insurers or guarantors: Only the information required for eligibility, authorization, claims, or settlement processing is shared.
  • With payment processors: Payment data may be processed through approved third-party payment providers.
  • With regulators or lawful authorities: Only where law, regulation, or patient safety obligations require it.
  • With technology providers: Only under contractual safeguards and security controls suitable for healthcare data.

We do not sell patient, staff, or healthcare operational data for advertising purposes.

5. Security controls

  • Role-based access control and separation of duties
  • Transport encryption, secure credential handling, and protected administrative access
  • Audit logging for sensitive clinical, billing, and pharmacy actions
  • Review controls for AI-assisted recommendations and workflow drafts
  • Retention and deletion practices aligned to clinical, financial, and legal requirements

6. Data retention

Retention periods depend on the type of record and the legal or operational reason for keeping it. Patient records, billing records, payroll records, pharmacy logs, and audit trails may each have different retention periods based on law, policy, payer requirements, or clinical safety needs.

7. Your rights

Where applicable under local law and institutional policy, individuals may have rights to:

  • Request access to personal data held about them
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Request restriction of certain forms of processing
  • Request deletion where there is no overriding legal or clinical reason to retain the data
  • Raise concerns about improper access, disclosure, or AI-assisted processing

8. AI governance and review

Organizations using MediHub HMS should maintain governance for AI features, including named oversight, approved use cases, quality review, user training, and clear escalation paths when AI suggestions appear unsafe, incomplete, or misleading.

9. Contact

For privacy or data-handling questions relating to MediHub HMS, contact your facility administrator or the platform support contact configured for your deployment.
